New Orleans, Louisiana

New Orleans, affectionately known as the "Big Easy," is a vibrant city in Louisiana, renowned for its rich history, diverse culture, and unique culinary landscape. Steeped in a blend of French, African, Spanish, and American influences, it's famous for its annual Mardi Gras celebration, a colorful festival filled with parades and masquerade balls. The city's historic heart, the French Quarter, charms visitors with its colonial-era architecture and lively Bourbon Street, known for jazz clubs, Cajun eateries, and raucous nightlife. Beyond the revelry, New Orleans is celebrated for its contributions to jazz music, with landmarks like Preservation Hall keeping the tradition alive. The city's spirit is further embodied in its cuisine, from Creole and Cajun dishes to beignets and po'boys, offering a taste of its multicultural heritage. Despite facing challenges, including the devastation of Hurricane Katrina in 2005, New Orleans has shown remarkable resilience, preserving its status as a beacon of cultural richness and diversity in the United States.

Honolulu, Hawaii

Honolulu, the vibrant capital city of Hawaii, is located on the island of Oahu and is a gateway to the Hawaiian archipelago. Known for its stunning landscapes, it is enveloped by the iconic Diamond Head crater and bordered by the beautiful Pacific Ocean. The city is a blend of traditional Hawaiian culture and modern American life, offering a unique experience to visitors and residents alike. Waikiki, its famous neighborhood, is known for its crescent-shaped beach lined with palm trees and high-rise hotels, offering a plethora of activities from surfing to sunbathing. Honolulu is also home to historic sites such as the Pearl Harbor National Memorial, which provides a poignant look at an important part of U.S. history. The city's culinary scene reflects its diverse cultural influences, showcasing a fusion of Asian, Polynesian, and American cuisines. Honolulu's year-round warm climate, coupled with its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, makes it a popular destination for tourists seeking a tropical escape with a rich historical and cultural depth.

I visited Hawaii with some friends, and the beauty of the state totally exceeded all my expectations. This picture was taken in March 2022.

Hot Springs, Arkansas

Hot Springs, nestled in the Ouachita Mountains, is a unique city known for its naturally heated springs that have been a draw for centuries. The heart of the city is the Hot Springs National Park, famed for its ancient thermal springs, beautiful mountain views, and historic Bathhouse Row, a collection of architecturally stunning bathhouses from the 19th and early 20th centuries, many of which now serve as museums and cultural venues. This city, often referred to as "America's First Resort," was historically a haven for health and relaxation, attracting visitors from around the world to its therapeutic waters. In addition to its spa heritage, Hot Springs boasts a charming downtown area with art galleries, shops, and restaurants. The surrounding area offers abundant outdoor activities like hiking, boating, and fishing in the picturesque Ouachita Lake and National Forest. Hot Springs also has a rich history in American gangster lore and was once a popular hideout for infamous figures like Al Capone, adding a layer of intriguing history to its already diverse character.

I visited Arkansas with some friends for a short weekend getaway. It was blast hiking, getting in the hot springs, and learning about the town's rich history. This picture was taken in February 2022.

New York, New York

New York City, affectionately dubbed "The Big Apple," stands as a beacon of urban culture and energy, and is the most populous city in the United States. It's a global center for finance, arts, culture, and fashion, boasting an unmistakable skyline, with iconic structures like the Empire State Building, the Chrysler Building, and the Statue of Liberty. This sprawling metropolis is composed of five distinct boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, The Bronx, and Staten Island, each offering its own unique flavor and cultural experiences.

New York's cultural landscape is as diverse as its population. It hosts world-renowned museums like the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the Museum of Modern Art, and the American Museum of Natural History. The city is also a major center for media, with major broadcasters and publications operating from here. Culinary experiences in New York range from street food vendors selling iconic New York hot dogs and pretzels to upscale dining experiences in Michelin-starred restaurants. Neighborhoods like Chinatown, Little Italy, and Harlem each offer their own unique and authentic culinary adventures.

Renowned for its non-stop energy and "city that never sleeps" persona, New York City is a melting pot of cultures and ideas, attracting dreamers, artists, entrepreneurs, and travelers from around the world. Its influence on commerce, finance, media, art, fashion, research, technology, education, and entertainment sets it apart as not just a pivotal American city, but a key player on the global stage. This dynamic city continues to be a symbol of the American Dream, offering endless opportunities amidst its towering skyscrapers and bustling streets.

I visited NYC for the first time over Memorial weekend in 2022. The food, musems, and diversity of people were all so impressive. My friends and I walked at least 20000 steps every day! I will definitely go back one day. This photo was taken in May 2022.

Seattle, Washington

Seattle, often referred to as the "Emerald City" due to its lush evergreen forests, is a vibrant and dynamic city known for its rich cultural heritage and innovative spirit. Nestled between Puget Sound and Lake Washington, it is famous for its stunning natural scenery, with the majestic Olympic and Cascade Mountains framing the skyline. This bustling metropolis is the largest city in the Pacific Northwest region of the United States and serves as a hub for technology, commerce, and the arts.

Seattle's iconic Space Needle, a legacy of the 1962 World's Fair, offers panoramic views of the cityscape and its natural surroundings. The city is also home to Pike Place Market, one of the oldest continuously operated public farmers' markets in the country, known for its vibrant atmosphere and diverse array of vendors. The waterfront area, with its piers and parks, is a popular spot for both locals and tourists, offering stunning views and an array of seafood restaurants.

Seattle is a leader in technology and innovation, home to tech giants like Amazon and Microsoft, making it a center for cutting-edge research and development. This technological influence has shaped the city's economy and culture, drawing a diverse and educated workforce.

Environmentally conscious, Seattle is known for its progressive policies and commitment to sustainability. The city's residents are known for their love of the outdoors, with abundant opportunities for hiking, biking, and boating in nearby natural areas like Mount Rainier National Park and the San Juan Islands.

I moved to Seattle in the beginnging of 2023 for a Masters Program at Northeastern University. During my time here, I have made many friends, went on many hikes, and have enjoyed the Pacific Northwest to the fullest! This picture was taken in October 2023.
